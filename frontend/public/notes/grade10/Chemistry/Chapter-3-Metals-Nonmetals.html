Chapter 3 - Metals and Non-metals

Class X Chemistry | CBSE

Metals

Metals are elements that form positive ions by losing electrons. They have the following characteristics:

  • Lustrous (shiny)
  • Good conductors of heat and electricity
  • Malleable (can be hammered into sheets)
  • Ductile (can be drawn into wires)
  • High melting and boiling points
  • Sonorous (produce sound)
Examples: Iron, Copper, Aluminum, Gold, Silver, Zinc

Non-metals

Non-metals are elements that form negative ions by gaining electrons. They have opposite properties to metals:

  • Non-lustrous (dull)
  • Poor conductors of heat and electricity
  • Brittle (break easily)
  • Low melting and boiling points
  • Non-sonorous
Examples: Oxygen, Nitrogen, Carbon, Sulfur, Chlorine, Phosphorus

Reactivity of Metals

Metals react with oxygen, water, and acids. More reactive metals displace less reactive metals from their compounds.

Reactivity Series

The order of reactivity of common metals: K > Na > Ca > Mg > Al > Zn > Fe > Cu > Hg > Ag > Au

Comparison of Metals and Non-metals

Property Metals Non-metals
Conductivity Good Poor (except graphite)
Malleability Malleable Brittle
Luster Lustrous Non-lustrous
Density Generally high Generally low

Key Takeaways

  • ✓ Metals: Good conductors, malleable, ductile
  • ✓ Non-metals: Poor conductors, brittle
  • ✓ Reactivity series determines displacement
  • ✓ Metals form cations; non-metals form anions
