Chapter 2 - Acids, Bases and Salts

Class X Chemistry | CBSE

Acids

Acids are compounds that produce H⁺ ions in water. They taste sour and turn blue litmus paper red.

  • Examples: HCl, H₂SO₄, HNO₃, Acetic acid
  • pH < 7

Bases

Bases are compounds that produce OH⁻ ions in water. They taste bitter and turn red litmus paper blue.

  • Examples: NaOH, KOH, Ca(OH)₂, Ammonia
  • pH > 7

pH Scale

pH = -log[H⁺]
pH < 7: Acidic
pH = 7: Neutral
pH > 7: Basic

Salts

Salts are compounds formed when an acid reacts with a base. They conduct electricity in aqueous solution.

Neutralization Reaction: Acid + Base → Salt + Water
Example: HCl + NaOH → NaCl + H₂O

Properties of Acids and Bases

  • Acids: Sour taste, turn blue litmus red, corrosive
  • Bases: Bitter taste, turn red litmus blue, slippery
  • Salts: Various properties depending on composition

Indicators

Indicators are substances that change color in acidic or basic solutions. Common indicators include litmus, methyl orange, and phenolphthalein.

Key Takeaways

  • ✓ Acids produce H⁺ ions (pH < 7)
  • ✓ Bases produce OH⁻ ions (pH > 7)
  • ✓ Neutralization: Acid + Base → Salt + Water
  • ✓ Indicators detect acids and bases
