Carbon - The Versatile Element

Carbon (atomic number 6) is one of the most important elements because it forms the backbone of all living organisms.

Allotropes of Carbon

  • Diamond: Hardest form, used in cutting tools
  • Graphite: Soft, conducts electricity, used in pencils
  • Buckminsterfullerene (C₆₀): Sports ball-shaped molecule
  • Amorphous Carbon: Coal, charcoal, soot

Organic Compounds

Organic compounds contain carbon and are typically compounds of carbon with hydrogen, oxygen, nitrogen, or other elements.

Types of Organic Compounds:
  • Alkanes (C_nH₂ₙ₊₂): Saturated hydrocarbons
  • Alkenes (C_nH₂ₙ): Unsaturated hydrocarbons
  • Alkynes (C_nH₂ₙ₋₂): Triple bonded hydrocarbons

Functional Groups

  • Hydroxyl Group (-OH): Found in alcohols and carboxylic acids
  • Carbonyl Group (C=O): Found in aldehydes and ketones
  • Carboxyl Group (-COOH): Found in carboxylic acids

Homologous Series

A group of organic compounds with the same functional group, differing by CH₂ units, showing gradual change in properties.

Examples:
Methane (CH₄), Ethane (C₂H₆), Propane (C₃H₈), Butane (C₄H₁₀)
(Homologous series of alkanes)

Isomerism

Compounds with the same molecular formula but different structural arrangements are called isomers.

Key Takeaways

  • ✓ Carbon has multiple allotropes
  • ✓ Organic compounds contain carbon
  • ✓ Functional groups determine compound properties
  • ✓ Homologous series show gradual property change
