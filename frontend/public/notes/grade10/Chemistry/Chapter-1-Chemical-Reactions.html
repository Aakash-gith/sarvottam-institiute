Chemical Reactions

A chemical reaction is a process where one or more substances (reactants) are transformed into different substances (products).

Types of Chemical Reactions

  • Combination Reaction: Two or more substances combine to form a single product
  • Decomposition Reaction: A single substance breaks down into two or more products
  • Displacement Reaction: An element displaces another element from a compound
  • Double Displacement Reaction: Two compounds exchange ions to form new compounds
Example: Combustion of carbon: C + O₂ → CO₂ (Combination reaction)

Chemical Equations

A chemical equation is a representation of a chemical reaction using chemical formulas and symbols.

General Form:
Reactant → Product
Example: 2H₂ + O₂ → 2H₂O

Balancing Chemical Equations

In a balanced equation, the number of atoms of each element is the same on both sides.

Key Point: Mass is conserved in chemical reactions (Law of Conservation of Mass)

Oxidation and Reduction

  • Oxidation: Loss of electrons or gain of oxygen
  • Reduction: Gain of electrons or loss of oxygen

Key Takeaways

  • ✓ Chemical reactions transform substances
  • ✓ Four types of chemical reactions
  • ✓ Chemical equations must be balanced
  • ✓ Oxidation and reduction occur together
