Introduction to Magnetism

Magnetism is a fundamental force in nature. When electric current flows through a conductor, it creates a magnetic field around it. This phenomenon is called the magnetic effect of electric current.

Key Point: Hans Christian Ørsted discovered in 1820 that an electric current produces a magnetic field.

Magnetic Field

A magnetic field is a region of space where a magnetic force can be experienced. The strength and direction are represented by magnetic field lines.

Magnetic Field Due to Current

  • Straight conductor: Magnetic field forms concentric circles around the conductor
  • Solenoid: Magnetic field is uniform inside and concentrated
  • Circular coil: Strong magnetic field at the center
Right-Hand Thumb Rule:
If thumb points in direction of current, fingers curl in direction of magnetic field.

Electromagnetic Induction

When a conductor moves in a magnetic field, an EMF is induced in it. This is the principle behind generators and electric motors.

Key Takeaways

  • ✓ Electric current produces magnetic field
  • ✓ Right-hand thumb rule for direction
  • ✓ Solenoid has uniform magnetic field
  • ✓ Moving conductor in field generates EMF
