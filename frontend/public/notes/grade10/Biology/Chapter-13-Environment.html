What is the Environment?

The environment comprises the physical (abiotic) and living (biotic) components surrounding organisms.

Ecosystem

An ecosystem is a functional unit consisting of living organisms and their physical environment interacting together.

Food Chain and Food Web

  • Food Chain: Linear sequence: Producer → Consumer → Decomposer
  • Food Web: Network of interconnected food chains
Energy Flow: Flows from producers to consumers (unidirectional)

Trophic Levels

  • Producers: Green plants (autotrophs)
  • Primary Consumers: Herbivores
  • Secondary Consumers: Carnivores
  • Tertiary Consumers: Top carnivores

Environmental Issues

  • Deforestation: Loss of forests
  • Pollution: Air, water, soil contamination
  • Climate Change: Global warming
  • Biodiversity Loss: Extinction of species

Conservation

Efforts to protect and restore natural resources and ecosystems.

  • National parks and wildlife sanctuaries
  • Waste management and recycling
  • Renewable energy sources

Sustainable Development

Development that meets present needs without compromising future generations' ability to meet theirs.

Key Takeaways

  • ✓ Ecosystem: Living + Physical environment
  • ✓ Food chain: Producer → Consumer → Decomposer
  • ✓ Trophic levels show energy transfer
  • ✓ Conservation protects biodiversity
