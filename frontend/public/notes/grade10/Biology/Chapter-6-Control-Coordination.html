Introduction to Control and Coordination

Control and coordination in living organisms enable them to respond to their environment and maintain internal balance.

Nervous System

The nervous system controls and coordinates various body functions through electrical signals.

  • Central Nervous System (CNS): Brain and spinal cord
  • Peripheral Nervous System (PNS): Nerves and ganglia

Neurons

Neurons are the functional units of the nervous system. They transmit electrical and chemical signals.

Structure: Cell body, dendrites, and axon

Reflex Arc

A reflex arc is the pathway of nerve impulses for a reflex action. It doesn't involve the brain initially.

Endocrine System

The endocrine system controls body functions through hormones (chemical messengers).

  • Pituitary gland, thyroid, pancreas, adrenal glands
  • Hormones regulate growth, reproduction, metabolism

Plant Hormones (Auxins)

Plant hormones control growth, flowering, fruiting, and response to stimuli.

Plant Movements

  • Phototropism: Movement towards light
  • Geotropism: Movement influenced by gravity
  • Hydrotropism: Movement towards water

Key Takeaways

  • ✓ CNS: Brain and spinal cord
  • ✓ PNS: Peripheral nerves
  • ✓ Neurons transmit signals
  • ✓ Hormones coordinate body functions
