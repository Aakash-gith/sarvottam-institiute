Chapter 7 - How do Organisms Reproduce?

Class X Biology | CBSE

What is Reproduction?

Reproduction is the process by which organisms produce offspring, ensuring continuity of species.

Types of Reproduction

  • Asexual Reproduction: Single parent, identical offspring (clones)
  • Sexual Reproduction: Two parents, genetically diverse offspring

Asexual Reproduction Methods

  • Binary Fission: Cell divides into two (bacteria)
  • Budding: New organism develops from parent (hydra)
  • Regeneration: Body part regrows (starfish)
  • Vegetative Propagation: Plants reproduce from vegetative parts

Sexual Reproduction in Plants

Involves male and female gametes. Occurs in the flower.

  • Male Part: Stamen (produces pollen)
  • Female Part: Pistil (produces ovule)
  • Pollination: Transfer of pollen to stigma
  • Fertilization: Fusion of male and female gametes

Sexual Reproduction in Animals

Involves gamete production, fertilization, and embryonic development.

Puberty

The period of life when individuals become capable of sexual reproduction. Usually occurs between ages 12-18.

Changes During Puberty: Physical growth, development of reproductive organs, hormonal changes

Human Reproduction

  • Male reproductive system: Testes, penis, vas deferens
  • Female reproductive system: Ovaries, fallopian tubes, uterus
  • Menstrual cycle: ~28 days

Key Takeaways

  • ✓ Asexual: Single parent, clones
  • ✓ Sexual: Two parents, genetic diversity
  • ✓ Pollination and fertilization in plants
  • ✓ Puberty enables sexual reproduction
