Chapter 8 - Heredity and Evolution

Class X Biology | CBSE

Heredity

Heredity is the transmission of genetic traits from parents to offspring through genes.

Mendel's Laws of Inheritance

  • Law of Segregation: Alleles separate during gamete formation
  • Law of Independent Assortment: Different genes assort independently

Genes and DNA

Genes are units of heredity made of DNA. DNA (deoxyribonucleic acid) carries genetic information.

DNA Structure: Double helix with base pairs (A-T, G-C)

Chromosomes

Thread-like structures containing genes. Humans have 46 chromosomes (23 pairs).

Evolution

Evolution is the process of gradual change in populations of organisms over time.

Theory of Natural Selection

Proposed by Charles Darwin. Organisms with favorable traits are more likely to survive and reproduce.

Evidence for Evolution

  • Fossil records
  • Comparative anatomy
  • DNA similarities
  • Vestigial structures

Speciation

The process by which new species arise from existing species through adaptation and isolation.

Key Takeaways

  • ✓ Heredity transmits traits through genes
  • ✓ DNA carries genetic information
  • ✓ Natural selection favors favorable traits
  • ✓ Evolution creates new species over time
